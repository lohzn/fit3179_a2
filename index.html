Visualizing Japan's Earthquakes: 2016-2018

Japan’s Earthquakes: Where and How Big?

This symbol map shows the location and magnitude of significant earthquakes (greater than 4 on the Richter scale) that occurred in Japan from 2016 to 2018. The size of the circle represents the magnitude of the earthquake, while the color represents the depth of the earthquake. The darker the color, the deeper the earthquake. We can see that most of the earthquakes are concentrated along the coastlines of Japan, especially in the south and east regions. This is because Japan is located on the Pacific Ring of Fire, where several tectonic plates meet and cause frequent seismic activity.

Japan’s Monthly Earthquake Frequency: When and How Many?

This bar chart shows the number of significant earthquakes that occurred in each month combined from 2016 to 2018. We can see that there is no clear seasonal pattern in the frequency of earthquakes, as they occur randomly throughout the year. However, we can notice some peaks and troughs in certain months. For example, September had the highest number of earthquakes (201), followed by November (158) and April (157). These months correspond to some major earthquakes that hit Japan, such as the Tomakomai earthquake in April 2018 and theHokkaido earthquake in September 2018.

Japan’s Yearly Earthquake Frequency: A Colorful Perspective

This heatmap shows another way of visualizing the frequency of significant earthquakes in each month from 2016 to 2018. The color intensity represents the number of earthquakes, with darker colors indicating higher numbers. We can see that this visualization confirms what we observed in the bar chart, that there is no clear seasonal pattern in the frequency of earthquakes, but there are some months with higher or lower numbers of earthquakes. We can also compare the frequency of earthquakes across different years by looking at the rows of the heatmap.

Japan’s Earthquake Depth vs Magnitude: How Deep and How Strong?

This line chart above shows the relationship between the depth and magnitude of significant earthquakes 2016, 2017 and 2018. The x-axis represents the depth of the earthquake, while the y-axis represents the magnitude of the earthquake. The color of the line represents the year of the earthquake. We can see that there is a negative correlation between the depth and magnitude of earthquakes, but again it is not very dicernable, meaning that the deeper the earthquake, the lower the magnitude. This is because deeper earthquakes release less energy than shallow earthquakes, as they have to travel through more layers of rock before reaching the surface. Moreover, we can see that there is a lot of congestion towards lower depths, indicating that many earthquakes are closer to the mainland. This implies that they are more dangerous and civilians need to be alert and prepared for potential disasters.
