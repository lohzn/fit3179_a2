Visualizing Earthquake Epicenters in Japan

This symbol map displays significant earthquakes (magnitude > 4) in Japan from 2016 to 2018. Circle size indicates magnitude, while color represents earthquake depth, from shallow to deep. Most earthquakes cluster along Japan's coastlines, particularly the south and east regions due to Japan's location on the Pacific Ring of Fire.

Frequency of Significant Earthquakes

This bar chart presents the total number of significant earthquakes each month from 2016 to 2018. There is no clear seasonal pattern, with earthquakes occurring randomly throughout the year. Some notable peaks include September (201), November (158), and April (157), coinciding with major earthquakes like the Tomakomai earthquake in April 2018 and Hokkaido earthquake in September 2018.

Unveiling the Magnitude Distribution of Earthquakes

The histogram illustrates the distribution of earthquake magnitudes in Japan from 2016 to 2018. The x-axis represents magnitude ranges, and the y-axis shows the count of earthquakes falling within each range. The histogram provides an overview of the frequency and distribution of different magnitudes.

Exploring the Correlation Between Earthquake Depth and Magnitude

The heatmap depicts the correlation between earthquake depth and magnitude over the years. Deeper earthquakes tend to have lower magnitudes, indicating that they release less energy while traveling through multiple rock layers. The heatmap reveals congestion at lower depths , highlighting the higher concentration of potentially dangerous earthquakes closer to the mainland . Public awareness and preparedness for potential disasters become crucial.
